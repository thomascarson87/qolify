Heat-Island Reduction

By replacing non-porous heat-absorbing surfaces like asphalt with expansive organic canopy, the site drastically lowers its contribution to the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect. The mature trees intercept solar radiation before it reaches the ground, storing latent heat in their biomass rather than reflecting it back into the atmosphere.

Air Quality Enrichment

A dense canopy serves as a biological particulate filter. Large leaf surface areas capture airborne pollutants and CO2, while simultaneously releasing oxygen and volatile organic compounds that foster a healthier breathing environment. Residents benefit from measurably lower nitrogen dioxide (NO2) concentrations compared to adjacent blocks.
